The Rise of PFAS-Free Food Packaging: What Brands Need to Know in 2026

Published: July 10, 2026 | Category: Sustainability

The regulatory landscape for food-contact packaging is undergoing its most significant transformation in decades. At the center of this shift are PFAS �?per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances �?commonly referred to as "forever chemicals" due to their persistence in the environment and the human body.

Why PFAS Matters in Food Packaging

PFAS have been widely used in food packaging �?particularly in grease-proof papers, pizza boxes, and takeout containers �?to repel oil and water. However, mounting scientific evidence linking PFAS exposure to health risks including cancer, thyroid disease, and immune system effects has prompted a global regulatory crackdown.

As of 2026, over 30 U.S. states have enacted or proposed PFAS restrictions in food packaging. The EU is moving toward a comprehensive ban under its Chemicals Strategy for Sustainability.

What This Means for Brands

If your business uses disposable food packaging �?whether you're a coffee chain, restaurant group, or CPG brand �?PFAS compliance is no longer a future consideration. It's a present-day requirement in many markets, and the trend is only accelerating.

Key actions brands should take now:

  • Audit your current packaging supply chain �?request PFAS-free certifications from all suppliers
  • Transition to certified PFAS-free alternatives �?such as plant-based coatings and uncoated kraft papers
  • Update your marketing �?"PFAS-Free" is becoming a powerful differentiator on shelves and menus
  • Stay ahead of regulations �?monitor legislation in your key markets to ensure continuous compliance

Jiufu's PFAS-Free Commitment

At Jiufu Packaging, all our food-contact paper cups, bowls, and bags are manufactured PFAS-free as standard. We use innovative plant-based barrier coatings that deliver the same grease and moisture resistance without harmful chemicals. Our PFAS-free products are already trusted by brands across North America and Europe.

Paper vs. Plastic: Why the Global Shift to Paper Packaging Is Accelerating

Published: June 10, 2026 | Category: Industry Trends

The global packaging industry is in the midst of a fundamental transformation. Paper-based packaging �?once considered the "traditional" choice �?is now the future, driven by a powerful convergence of regulation, consumer sentiment, and technological innovation.

The Numbers Behind the Shift

The global paper packaging market was valued at approximately $380 billion in 2025 and is projected to grow at a CAGR of 4.8% through 2030. Meanwhile, single-use plastic packaging faces increasing restrictions worldwide:

  • The EU Single-Use Plastics Directive has banned 10 categories of single-use plastic items
  • Over 127 countries have implemented some form of plastic bag regulation
  • Major corporations including Starbucks, McDonald's, and Unilever have committed to 100% recyclable or compostable packaging by 2030

Why Paper Wins

Paper offers advantages that align with both regulatory requirements and consumer preferences:

  • Renewable: Made from trees �?a renewable resource when sourced from certified forests (FSC)
  • Recyclable: Paper has the highest recycling rate of any packaging material (68% in the US, 82% in Europe)
  • Biodegradable: Unlike plastic, paper decomposes naturally without leaving microplastics
  • Brand perception: Studies show 73% of consumers are willing to pay more for sustainable packaging

The Innovation Factor

Modern paper packaging is not your grandfather's brown bag. Advances in barrier coatings, forming technology, and printing have made paper cups, bowls, and bags competitive with plastic on performance while being far superior on sustainability. EU PPWR Regulation 2026: How It Benefits Paper Packaging Manufacturers

Published: July 15, 2026 | Category: Policy

The European Union's Packaging and Packaging Waste Regulation (PPWR), adopted in 2025 and taking full effect in 2026, represents the most ambitious packaging reform in history. For paper packaging manufacturers, this regulation is a game-changer in the best possible way.

Key Provisions Favoring Paper Packaging

The PPWR sets binding targets for packaging waste reduction, recycled content, and reuse. Specifically:

  • Recyclability mandate: All packaging placed on the EU market must be recyclable by 2030. Paper already achieves 82% recycling rates in Europe.
  • Single-use plastic restrictions: Bans on single-use plastic packaging for fruits/vegetables, mini hotel toiletries, and food consumed on-premises directly increase demand for paper alternatives.
  • Compostability requirements: Certain applications (tea bags, coffee pods, sticky labels on produce) must be compostable, favoring paper-based solutions.
  • Mandatory recycled content: Starting 2030, plastic packaging must contain minimum recycled content percentages, making paper a more cost-competitive alternative.
Analysts project the PPWR will shift €8-12 billion in packaging spending from plastic to paper-based solutions across the EU by 2030.

Opportunities for Your Business

For custom paper packaging manufacturers, this creates unprecedented demand. European brands are urgently seeking PPWR-compliant suppliers who can provide FSC-certified, fully recyclable paper cups, bags, boxes, and bowls at competitive prices.

At Jiufu Packaging, all our products meet or exceed PPWR requirements. Our FSC certification, PFAS-free materials, and FDA-compliant production processes position us as an ideal partner for EU-bound brands.

China's Plastic Restriction Policy: Driving Global Demand for Eco-Friendly Packaging

Published: July 8, 2026 | Category: Policy

China's multi-phase plastic restriction policy, launched in 2020 and continuously strengthened through 2026, is reshaping the global packaging landscape. As the world's largest packaging producer, China's domestic policy shifts have outsized effects on global supply and demand.

Policy Evolution: 2020-2026

The policy has escalated through several phases:

  • Phase 1 (2020-2022): Ban on non-degradable plastic bags in major cities, single-use plastic straws nationwide
  • Phase 2 (2023-2025): Extended bans to all urban areas, added restrictions on disposable plastic tableware in food delivery
  • Phase 3 (2025-2026): Comprehensive restrictions on single-use plastics across all sectors, with mandatory recycled content targets for permitted plastic packaging

Export Implications

China's policy creates a dual advantage for exporters of paper packaging:

  • Supply chain maturity: Domestic demand has driven massive investment in paper packaging technology, making Chinese paper packaging among the most advanced and cost-competitive globally
  • Innovation spillover: Technologies developed for China's domestic market (advanced barrier coatings, molded pulp forming) create export-ready solutions
  • Raw material advantage: China's bamboo and agricultural fiber supply chains provide sustainable alternatives to wood pulp

30 U.S. States Ban PFAS in Food Packaging: Complete Compliance Guide

Published: June 30, 2026 | Category: Policy

As of mid-2026, over 30 U.S. states have enacted or proposed legislation restricting PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances) in food packaging. This state-by-state regulatory patchwork creates compliance challenges but also significant market opportunities for manufacturers who get ahead of the curve.

State-by-State Overview

Key states with active PFAS bans in food packaging include:

  • California (AB 1200): Effective January 2023, bans PFAS in plant-based food packaging. The market-shaping regulation that set the national trend.
  • New York: Effective December 2022, with enforcement ramping up in 2024-2025.
  • Washington, Vermont, Connecticut, Maine, Minnesota: All with active bans, with varying phase-in schedules through 2026.
  • Colorado, Maryland, Rhode Island, Hawaii: Enacted and phasing in enforcement.
The U.S. FDA has also announced a voluntary market phase-out of PFAS in food packaging, with major manufacturers committing to complete elimination by end of 2025.

Compliance Checklist for Importers

If you're a brand importing food packaging into the U.S., your supplier must meet these minimum requirements:

  • PFAS-free certification: Third-party laboratory testing documentation
  • FDA 21 CFR compliance: For food-contact substances
  • State-level documentation: Varies by state; California requires specific declarations
  • Chain of custody: Traceability from raw material to finished product
